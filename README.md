Disadvantages of AI in Education

The idea of using Artificial Intelligence is really interesting. We have to think about the problems it can cause. Artificial Intelligence can bring some issues that we need to deal with. These issues are about what's right and wrong how the technology works and how it affects people. We need to be very careful when we start using Artificial Intelligence.

Using Artificial Intelligence can be a risk if we do not think about these things. We have to make sure we are managing the risks of Artificial Intelligence carefully. Artificial Intelligence is something that we need to be careful, with.

1. Ethical Risks and Privacy Violations: People really care about privacy. The thing is, users of these applications often have no idea how much of their information is being collected. There is a risk that student ideas can be basically stalked by these conversational chatbots.. Then there is the possibility of data breaches, which is very scary. What is also really bad is that these algorithms can be biased against groups of people like because of their gender or race. This means some students can be treated unfairly and it just makes the existing problems of inequality even worse. The algorithmic applications can really hurt students and the privacy violations are a concern for these students and their user experience, with the algorithmic applications.

2. The "Black Box" and Lack of Explainability: A big problem with learning models is that they cannot explain how they work. When an Artificial Intelligence solves a problem but cannot tell us how it did it this is like a "Black Box". The "Black Box" is a problem for education because students do not understand the idea behind it. They just see the answer. They do not know how the Artificial Intelligence got that answer. This means the "Black Box" of the Artificial Intelligence is not really helping students learn. The "Black Box" is a problem because it does not teach students the basics of the subject. The deep learning models are, like a "Black Box" that solves problems but the "Black Box" does not explain the deep learning models.

3. We Lose Out On Human Connection and Basic People Skills: Artificial Intelligence cannot replace the support and social things that human teachers give us like understanding what our body language means or giving us real encouragement when we need it. If we use Artificial Intelligence much it might hurt our ability to develop basic people skills like being a good leader thinking creatively and working well with others, which are things that we learn best when we interact with other people, in person.

4. AIED Colonialism and Commercialization: People're getting worried about AIED colonialism. This is when companies from the Global North send their computer models to parts of the world. These models are trained in ways and might push out the local ways of teaching.

Also big technology companies are changing the way they do business. They are creating platforms and renting out their services. This might mean they care more about making money and collecting data than, about helping people learn and think in a way. The issue is that AIED colonialism and commercialization can affect how people learn and think. AIED is being used in different cultures and AIED is changing the way people teach and learn.

5. Costs of putting Artificial Intelligence in place and unfairness: The Artificial Intelligence system is very expensive to set up and maintain. This includes the cost of computers and other equipment. Many schools, those in rural areas or developing countries cannot afford this. This means that only rich schools will be able to use Artificial Intelligence. The poor schools will be left behind. This is not fair. It will make the gap, between rich and poor schools even bigger.
